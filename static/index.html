    Curious Meals - Meal Planning - Tutorial

    Curious Meals is a tool to help you find your recipes and plan great healty meals. See license for use and privacy policy.

    Five Basic Parts

    Ingredients are the building blocks for dishes. Ingredients track the souce (vegan, vegetarian or animal) and the kind of food (Fruit, Vegetable, Protein, etc).

    Dishes are the bulding blocks for a menu. Dishes gather all the information of a recipe including a list of ingredients. You also record the balance of nutrients (Veggetables, Protein, Carbohydrate) to enable you to see how balanced your meal will be.

    Menus are a collection of dishes, you can see a menu at a glance on the right, or dig deeper to see a list of ingredients needed for all of the dishes. Menus allow you to see the overall nutritional balance of the meal you're preparing.

    Tags are words or phrases you can attach to ingredients and dishes. These enable you to search and organize your library to make it easy to find everything.

    Libraries are a collection of ingredients, dishes, menus and tags. You can share your library with other users. If you have access to other libraries, you can only look at them one at a time. Use the menu in the upper left to switch libraries.

    Getting Around

    The meal planner displays itself in three main areas that you can se right now; on the left are tabs to search or browse dishes and ingredients; on the right you can see the menu you're currently working with; in the center, where you are reading now, you can see detailed information about each item.

    Searching and Browsing

    On the left are a series of tab with icons for Search, Dishes, Ingredients and Tags.

    Search

    You can search by typing keywords into the search box and pressing the search button to its right. All items that match any word entered will appear below.

    You can filter your search by rating; only dishes with at least as many stars as you select will be displayed. Click on the star to set or clear a rating filter.

    You can also filter you search by tags; only items will appear that contain all of the tags that you enter. Type tags into the edit box below the Tags list and press enter. Hover the mouse over the tag and click theto remove it from the search.

    Dishes and Ingredients

    The two middle tabs list dishes and ingredients in alphabetical order. Click on the item to read it in more detail. Hover the mouse over the item to get a quick preview.

    Tags

    The tags tab shows a list of tags from your items. Clicking on the tag link will search for items with that tag.

    Menus

    On the right side you can select from your menus and see the content of the currently select menu. The menu "<New Menu>" is a special menu for you to use while you're constructing a menu. You can add and remove items from the menu and then use the Save button to save it with a new name. Named menus can be deleted and cloned.

    If you have a dish in the center pane of the meal planner, you can click the Add button to add the dish to the menu. You can also click and drag a link for any dish to the menu display to add it.

    Nutritional Balance

    The tools for nutritonal balance are roughly based on the FDA's guidelines that a balanced meal be close to half a plate of Fruits and Vegetables, a quarter Protein and a quarter Carbohydrates/grains.

    To help show this balance, you should enter information for each dish estimating that balance -- if a dish would satisfy the full serving (quarter plate) of protein, increase the Protein count to 1, if it would satisfy the full serving (half a plate) of Fruits and Vegetables, increase the Fuits a Vegetable count to 2.

    The menu display shows a pie chart for the nutritional balance of the menu plus a reference view of an ideal meal.

    Adding Content

    To add a new dish or ingredient to your library, click the Dish and Ingredient buttons in the upper left. A new item will be created and pulled up in edit view in the center of the page.

    As you edit, the item will be saved automatically -- if the save button us greyed and and says "Saved" you know all your content is safely stored on the server.

    Suggestions

    You can make notes about suggestions for each dish; use this if there's a common pairing of things that go well together, or if there's an alternative dish that is a good substitute.

    You can drag a link to a dish onto the center pane to create a suggestion, or you can type in the name of the dish. You will then be prompted to specify if this is a suggestion for an alternative or a dish that would be recommended to go with the original.
